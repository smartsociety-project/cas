An article such as a news article or piece of investigative report. Newspapers and magazines have articles of many different types and this is intended to cover them all (same as schema:Article).
A book (same as schema:Book).
A figure is a diagram or illustrative drawing (same as schema:ImageObject).
A dataset is a body of structured information describing some topic(s) of interest (same as schema:Dataset).
Code means computer programming source code. Example: Full (compile ready) solutions, code snippet samples, scripts, templates (same as schema:Code).
Slides are electronic presentations consisting of a series of separate pages or slides.
A Citation a reference to another creative work (especially in a scholarly work). (subtype of prov:Derivation).
An Author is the prov:Agent to which some creative work is attributed as its author.
A Reviewer a person who writes critical appraisals of creative works. It is the prov:Agent to which some creative work is attributed as its reviewer.
A Contributor a person who contributes to some creative work, but the nature of the contribution is not specific. It is the prov:Agent to which some creative work is attributed as its contributor.
A Funder a person or organization who financially supported some creative work. It is the prov:Agent to which some creative work is attributed as its funder.
An Editor is the prov:Agent to which some creative work is attributed as its editor.
A ScheduledSession is a planned period devoted to a particular activity (subtype of prov:Plan).
A PresentationActivity is an activity during which some creative work is presented to an audience (subtype of prov:Activity).
A table a set of facts or figures systematically displayed, especially in columns. (same as schema:Table).
A poster is a large, usually printed placard, bill, or announcement, often illustrated, that is posted to advertise or publicize something.
A tweet is a posting made on the social media website Twitter.
A BindingsDerivation is the transformation that led to some document given a set of bindings (subtype of prov:Derivation).
A mention is a reference to someone or something. e.g. a tweet mentions a presentation. (subtype of prov:Derivation).
An expansion is the transformation that lead to some document after expansion of a template (subtype of prov:Derivation).
